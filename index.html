Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What makes a password strong?
A strong password should have at least 12 characters, include uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols. Avoid using personal information or common words.
Q: How does CyberGuard calculate crack time?
We use entropy-based calculations considering password length, character variety, and modern computing power (assuming 1 billion guesses per second from a GPU cluster).
Q: Should I use password managers?
Yes! Password managers can generate and store complex, unique passwords for each of your accounts, significantly improving your overall security posture.
Q: Is CyberGuard storing my passwords?
No! All password analysis happens locally in your browser. We never transmit, store, or have access to your passwords. Your security is our priority.
Q: What is "search space" in password analysis?
Search space represents the total number of possible password combinations based on length and character set. A larger search space means more guesses are needed to crack the password.
